What is Special Need?

Any difficulties (such as a physical, emotional, behavioral, or learning disability or impairment) that causes an individual to require additional or specialized services or accommodations (such as in education or recreation)

Types

Physical – multiple sclerosis, allergies and asthma, juvenile arthritis, leukemia, muscular dystrophy, epilepsy.

Developmental – Down syndrome, autism, dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia, dyspraxia, aphasia or dysphasia, auditory processing disorder, visual processing disorder.

Behavioral / Emotional – obsessive compulsive disorder, dissociation, post-traumatic stress disorder, anxiety, depression, attention deficit (hyperactivity) disorder, bipolar.

Sensory-impaired – blindness, deaf or limited hearing, visually impaired.

Most common special needs that young children are diagnosed with:
  • Speech and/or language delays
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Cognitive delays
  • Social & emotional disorders
  • Learning differences / disabilities
Other essential info

MILD / MODERATE DISABILITIES

Specific learning disabilities, like struggling in reading or math, to speech impairment where the student needs speech therapy.

MODERATE / SEVERE DISABILITIES

Severe physical limitations, emotional disturbances or extreme developmental delays may require ongoing supervision.

AUTISM

Autism spectrum conditions range from mild to severe.
