What is Special Need?

Any difficulties (such as a physical, emotional, behavioral, or learning disability or impairment) that causes an individual to require additional or specialized services or accommodations (such as in education or recreation)

Types

There are four main categories of special needs:

Physical -
multiple sclerosis, allergies and asthma, juvenile arthritis, leukemia, muscular dystrophy, epilepsy.

Developmental -
Down syndrome, autism, dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia, dyspraxia, aphasia or dysphasia, auditory processing disorder, visual processing disorder.

Behavioral/emotional -
obsessive compulsive disorder, dissociation, post-traumatic stress disorder, anxiety, depression, attention deficit (hyperactivity) disorder, bipolar.

Sensory-impaired -
blindness, deaf or limited hearing, visually impaired.

Most common special needs that young children diagnose with are:
  • Speech and/or language delays.
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder.
  • Cognitive delays.
  • Social & emotional disorders.
  • Learning differences/disabilities
Other essential info

MILD/MODERATE DISABILITIES

Specific learning disabilities, like struggling in reading or math, to speech impairment where the student needs speech therapy for pronunciation issues. Even a physical disability, such as a hearing impairment or health impairment, can be mild.

MODERATE/SEVERE DISABILITIES

If a special student hinders them from being around others unaccompanied by a teacher, they could fall into the more severe disability category. These students might have disabilities ranging from severe physical limitations, emotional disturbances or extreme developmental delays.

AUTISM

Autism spectrum have cases that range from mild to severe.
